7 wonders of India

Description: India is a land of diversity, art, and culture and it is reflected in the Seven Wonders of India. Yes, while everyone knows about the Seven Wonders of the World, India has its own Seven Wonders to boast of and they are symbols of pride for the country. They reflect the heritage and rich history of the nation and its archives the greatness of the country as a whole. Here is a list of these very special seven wonders in India.

Taj Mahal

Taj Mahal

Description: The magnificent Taj Mahal in Agra. The mausoleum was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mamtaz Mahal. It is one of the Seven Wonders of the entire world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site as well. The mausoleum is made entirely in white marble and there is inlaid work. The huge building had real gemstones encrusted in the walls, which were later pillaged by intruders and invaders. The words of the Holy Quran are engraved on the walls while in the center of the mausoleum, the empress and the emperor lay side by side in eternal peace.

Hampi

Hampi

Description: Hampi is located by the great river Tungabhadra in Karnataka and happens to be the most famous of all the historical places in India, still more or less perfectly preserved. The historical ruins and relics are from medieval times and were built during the great Vijaynagar Empire. The ruins of Hampi are already a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the monuments and temples in the complex are breathtaking.

The Golden Temple

The Golden Temple

Description: This dazzling temple is the holy seat of the Sikhs all over the world. It is popularly known as the Golden Temple or the Durbar Sahib. The Holy Granth Sahib is kept here and the gold-plated temple has stunning copper domes and a body of pristine white marble. The motifs have Islamic influence and the floral patterns are intricate and exquisite. The place is very calm and serene and although the daily footfall is very high, everyone maintains discipline and offers their prayers with utmost serenity.

The Sun Temple

The Sun Temple

Description: The Sun Temple is located in Konark in Orissa and is a cluster of ancient temples with jaw-dropping artwork and sculptures. The stone structure is immense and it is hard to imagine how such intricate artwork was depicted here. The temple is built in the Kalinga style and was commissioned by King Narsimhadeva the First of the Ganga Dynasty. The most notable feature of the temple is the three statues of the Sun God where the sun rays hit at dawn, noon, and dusk. The sundial has 12 wheels which are pulled by seven horses and depict the time of the day.

Gomoteshwar Statue

Gomoteshwar Statue

Description: Gomoteshwar is a monolithic statue and it is dedicated to Lord Bahubali who is considered to be an important leader of the Jains. This huge statue is located in the Shravanabelagola region of Karnataka and happens to be one of the largest free-standing statues in the entire world. The statue looks very serene and calm and thousands of visitors come here every day. The statue is a staggering 160 feet tall and is a sight to behold. The temple of the statue holds the Mahamastakabhishek festival each year which happens once in 12 years.

Khajuraho

Khajuraho

Description: Khajuraho in Madhya Pradesh is known for its great and marvelous Hindu and Jain temples and because of their jaw-dropping statuettes. The artwork is spectacular and the sculptures depict romance, ove, and spirituality at the same time. It is a monument dedicated to artwork worldwide. There are several temples here like the Kendriya Mahadev Temple and the Devi Jagaambi Temple, along with the Chausath Yogini Temple, Chitragupta Temple, and Nandi Temple, among various others. A sound and light show takes place here which depicts the reign of the Chandela dynasty.
